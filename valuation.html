AVM vs CMA vs Appraisal

Automated values are only the starting point.

An AVM, or automated valuation model, uses public data, tax records, property details, and online market activity to estimate value. Sites like Zillow, Realtor.com, Redfin, Homes.com, and RPR can all show different numbers because each system weighs the data differently.


A true CMA compares your home against recent local sales, active competition, condition, upgrades, layout, neighborhood, and buyer demand. An appraisal is a lender-driven valuation completed by a licensed appraiser during a transaction.


Best use: AVM for a fast starting point. CMA for a realistic sale strategy. Appraisal for lender approval.
