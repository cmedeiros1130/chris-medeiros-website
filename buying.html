Buying Process

Looking to Buy

Buying in Rhode Island or Massachusetts does not need to feel confusing. Here is the simple path from pre-approval to closing.

01

Get clear on financing first

Before you spend time touring homes, the smart move is getting pre-approved. This gives you a realistic budget, shows sellers you are serious, and helps you move quickly when the right property hits the market.

02

Choose local representation

A strong buyer’s agent helps you understand contracts, pricing, deadlines, buyer representation, inspections, negotiations, and local Rhode Island customs from start to finish.

03

Define your search

We narrow down the towns, property types, monthly payment comfort zone, must-haves, and deal breakers so your search stays focused instead of overwhelming.

04

Tour homes with purpose

During showings, we look beyond the photos. Layout, condition, resale factors, neighborhood fit, and potential red flags all matter before you write an offer.

05

Write a strong offer

When the right home comes along, we review comparable sales, market activity, seller priorities, financing, deposits, inspection terms, and closing timeline to put together the best possible offer.

06

Negotiate strategically

A deal is not always only about price. Terms, timing, contingencies, credits, and seller needs can all play a role in getting your offer accepted while protecting your interests.

07

Complete inspections

After acceptance, inspections help you understand the true condition of the property. We review the results and discuss your options clearly before making any decisions.

08

Appraisal and loan approval

Your lender orders the appraisal and moves the file toward final approval. I stay in communication with the lender, attorney, and other side to keep the process on track.

09

Final walkthrough

Before closing, we walk through the property to confirm it is in the expected condition and that any agreed items have been addressed.

10

Closing day

Once the final documents are signed and the deed is recorded, you receive the keys and officially become the homeowner.
